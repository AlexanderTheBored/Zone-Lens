# ZoneGuide - Mandaue Flood Risk Analysis User Manual

## Overview
ZoneGuide is an interactive map application for analyzing flood risks and zoning regulations in Mandaue City, Philippines. This manual provides information about the city's zoning districts, flood risk zones, and map features.

---

## Flood Risk Zones

### CRITICAL RISK (Dark Red) - 6-9 feet flooding
**Water Depth:** Roof-level flooding (6-9 feet / 2nd floor to roof level)

**Affected Areas:**
1. **Paknaan-Umapad Overflow Corridor**
   - 190+ families regularly evacuated
   - Last incident: November 2025 (5,000+ evacuees)
   - Frequency: Every typhoon + sustained heavy rainfall

2. **Ibabao-Estancia Major Overflow**
   - Primary overflow point during river spill
   - Last incident: November 2025 (8,500+ evacuees)
   - Water depth exceeds rescue equipment capacity

3. **Casuntingan Damaged Riprap Zone**
   - 675 houses destroyed in December 2022 flood
   - Last incident: November 2025 (12,000+ evacuees)
   - Riprap damage allows massive overflow

4. **Maguikay-Tabok Footbridge Area**
   - Official overflow monitoring point
   - Bridge regularly submerges
   - Last incident: November 2025 (3,500+ evacuees)

**Recommendation:** Evacuation recommended during typhoons and heavy rainfall warnings.

---

### HIGH RISK (Red) - 2-8 feet flooding
**Water Depth:** Knee to 2nd floor flooding (2-8 feet)

**Affected Areas:**
1. **Alang-Alang Buffer Zone**
   - Adjacent to critical overflow areas
   - Sitio Pulang Bukid frequently affected
   - Depth: 2-4 feet (knee to waist)

2. **Tingub Extended Flood Plain**
   - Part of 27 officially designated flood-prone barangays
   - Depth: 1-3 feet (ankle to knee)
   - Frequency: Moderate to heavy rainfall

**Recommendation:** Monitor weather alerts and prepare evacuation plans.

---

### MODERATE RISK (Orange) - 6 inches to 2 feet
**Water Depth:** Street to knee-deep flooding

**Affected Areas:**
1. **Banilad-A.S. Fortuna Drainage Area**
   - M. Cuenco Ave experiences drainage issues
   - Rolling Hills area reported waist-deep flooding
   - Depth: 6 inches - 2 feet
   - Frequency: Heavy downpours and monsoon season

2. **Cabancalan-Canduman Catchment**
   - Talamban mini dam provides some flood mitigation
   - Depth: 3-12 inches (street flooding)
   - Frequency: Extreme weather events only

**Recommendation:** Avoid low-lying areas during heavy rainfall.

---

### LOW RISK (Yellow) - Under 6 inches
**Water Depth:** Minor street flooding (under 6 inches)

**Affected Areas:**
1. **Eastern Elevated Areas**
   - Higher elevation zones
   - Depth: < 6 inches
   - Frequency: Rare - only during extreme events

2. **Western Periphery Zone**
   - Distance from river overflow zones provides natural protection
   - Depth: < 3 inches
   - Frequency: Very rare

**Recommendation:** Generally safe, minimal precautions needed.

## Butuanon River

**Overview:**
- Length: 23 kilometers
- Source: Mountains → Mactan Channel
- Main flood source for Mandaue City

**River Path:**
Banilad → Cabancalan → Casuntingan → Tingub → Maguikay → Tabok → Ibabao → Alang-alang → Paknaan → Umapad

**Flood Characteristics:**
- Primary cause of flooding in Mandaue
- Overflow occurs during typhoons and sustained heavy rainfall
- Affects 27 officially designated flood-prone barangays

## Map Features & Layers

- Shows elevation with hillshading

- Helps identify flood sources

- Helps understand terrain

- Find addresses, buildings, and places of interest
- Search within Mandaue City boundaries
- Results show on map with markers

## How to Use the Map

### Viewing Zones
1. Use the **Project Filter** to see which areas allow specific developments:
   - View All Zones
   - Residential Projects
   - Industrial Facilities
   - Commercial Complexes

2. Click on any colored zone to see detailed information:
   - Zone name and type
   - Permitted uses
   - Flood risk level
   - Population/business data

### Analyzing Flood Risk
1. Toggle the **Flood Risk Zones** layer
2. Click on colored flood zones to see:
   - Risk level (Critical/High/Moderate/Low)
   - Typical water depth
   - Frequency of flooding
   - Last incident details

### Understanding Terrain
1. Enable **3D Terrain Map** to see elevation
2. Enable **3D Buildings** to see structures
3. Use **Elevation Lines** for precise topography
4. Enable **Water Bodies** to see rivers and streams

### Finding Locations
1. Use the search bar at the top
2. Type address, building name, or place
3. Click on search results to zoom to location
4. Marker will appear on selected location

## Common Questions

**Q: Which areas are safest from flooding?**
A: Eastern elevated areas and western periphery zones (shown in yellow on flood risk layer) have minimal flood risk.

**Q: Can I build a house in an industrial zone?**
A: No, residential houses are not permitted in Industrial (I-2) zones like Subangdaku-Tingub.

**Q: What should I do if I'm in a critical flood zone?**
A: Monitor weather alerts closely and evacuate when warnings are issued. Critical zones experience roof-level flooding during typhoons.

**Q: Which commercial areas have the highest flood risk?**
A: A.S. Fortuna Commercial Corridor and Maguikay-Tabok Commercial Strip both have high flood risk.

**Q: Where does most flooding come from?**
A: The Butuanon River is the primary flood source, causing overflow in adjacent barangays during heavy rainfall and typhoons.

## Frequently Asked Questions & Expected Answers

### Geographic Scope

**Q: Can you show zones outside of Mandaue?**
**A:** This map only covers Mandaue City, Philippines. I can't show areas outside Mandaue's boundaries.

**Q: What about Cebu City / Lapu-Lapu / other areas?**
**A:** This application is specifically designed for Mandaue City only. For information about other cities, you would need their respective zoning and flood risk maps.

**Q: Does this include Metro Cebu?**
**A:** No, this map is limited to Mandaue City only, not the entire Metro Cebu area.

### Zoning Questions

**Q: Can I build a factory in Banilad?**
**A:** No, Banilad is a Mixed-Use (MU-1) zone which allows houses, malls, and offices, but not factories. For industrial facilities, you need to look at the Subangdaku-Tingub Industrial Zone.

**Q: Where can I open a shopping mall?**
**A:** Shopping malls are permitted in Commercial (C-2), Industrial (I-2), and Mixed-Use (MU-1) zones. Good locations include A.S. Fortuna-Banilad, Maguikay-Tabok, Subangdaku-Tingub, and Banilad areas.

**Q: I want to build a house, which zones allow that?**
**A:** Houses are allowed in Residential (R-1), Commercial (C-2), and Mixed-Use (MU-1) zones. This includes Cabancalan-Canduman, Paknaan-Umapad, A.S. Fortuna-Banilad, Maguikay-Tabok, and Banilad areas.

**Q: What's the difference between R-1 and C-2 zones?**
**A:** Residential (R-1) zones only allow houses and community facilities. Commercial (C-2) zones allow both houses AND commercial establishments like malls and shops. R-1 is more restrictive to maintain residential character.

**Q: Can I build an apartment building in a residential zone?**
**A:** Residential (R-1) zones allow residential housing, which typically includes apartments and multi-family dwellings, but check specific building height and density regulations with the city planning office.

### Flood Risk Questions

**Q: What's the worst flooding area in Mandaue?**
**A:** The critical flood zones are Paknaan-Umapad, Ibabao-Estancia, Casuntingan, and Maguikay-Tabok. These areas experience 6-9 feet of flooding (roof-level) during typhoons and require regular evacuations.

**Q: Is Banilad safe from flooding?**
**A:** Banilad has moderate flood risk with 6 inches to 2 feet of flooding during heavy downpours and monsoon season. It's safer than critical zones but still experiences street to knee-deep flooding.

**Q: When was the last major flood?**
**A:** November 2025 saw 29,000+ people evacuated across multiple barangays. Before that, December 2022 had a devastating flood that destroyed 675 houses in Casuntingan.

**Q: How often does flooding occur?**
**A:** It varies by zone:
- Critical zones: Every typhoon and sustained heavy rainfall
- High risk zones: During moderate to heavy rainfall
- Moderate zones: Heavy downpours and monsoon season
- Low risk zones: Rare, only during extreme events

**Q: Should I buy property in Paknaan?**
**A:** Paknaan is in a critical flood zone with roof-level flooding during typhoons. 190+ families are regularly evacuated. Consider flood risk carefully and factor in flood mitigation costs, insurance, and safety concerns.

**Q: What causes the flooding?**
**A:** The Butuanon River (23km long) is the main flood source. During typhoons and heavy rainfall, the river overflows, affecting 27 flood-prone barangays along its path through Mandaue.
